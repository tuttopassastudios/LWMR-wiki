# Sontec EQ

## Summary

> [!abstract]
> The Sontec is a legendary parametric mastering EQ that has been a staple of high-end mastering studios for decades. Known for its extremely musical and transparent sound, it commands premium prices on the used market. The HS-1000 model is notorious for reliability issues, and community discussions reveal that many expensive service complaints stem from user error. The Sontec circuit has inspired numerous clones and derivatives.

## Key Characteristics

- Parametric mastering EQ with extremely musical and transparent operation
- The HS-1000 units are known to "explode" — reliability is a real concern
- Premium price point ($10,000+) puts it firmly in the mastering studio category
- User error is frequently the cause of service complaints, not always the unit itself
- Inspired numerous clone designs including GML and other mastering EQs
- Units require careful maintenance and knowledgeable technicians

## Use Cases

- High-end mastering chains
- Stereo mix bus EQ for subtle tonal shaping
- Any application requiring the most transparent and musical parametric EQ available

## Settings & Sweet Spots

- Designed for subtle, precision mastering moves — not for dramatic tone shaping
- Best used with conservative boost/cut amounts where the musicality of the curves matters most

## Common Mistakes

- Expecting reliability from the HS-1000 without a maintenance budget and a qualified technician
- Getting "mean and aggressive" with service complaints when the issue is user error
- Buying a Sontec without understanding the ongoing maintenance commitment
- Assuming any parametric EQ plugin can replicate the Sontec's musicality
