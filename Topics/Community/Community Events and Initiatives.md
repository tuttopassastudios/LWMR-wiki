# Community Events and Initiatives

## Summary
> [!abstract]
> The Live with Matt Rad Discord community has organically developed events, challenges, and milestones that strengthen the community beyond technical discussion. From mastering competitions to community profiles, these initiatives reflect the server's culture of mutual support and creative collaboration.

## Detail

### Server Milestones

Key moments in the community's history:

> [!quote] Discord Source
> **Author:** oaklandmatt — **Channel:** #general-talk (2026-02-07)
> From my journal five years ago yesterday: "I've been working with Hendrick and Iggy getting the discord server up, and just sent out a bunch of invites. It's cool to see the beginnings of a community happen."

- **February 2021** — Server founded by oaklandmatt
- **December 2021** — First major holiday message / community gratitude post (92 reactions)
- **2023** — Peak activity year across all channels (20,477 messages in #general-talk alone)
- **August 2024** — 2,000 member milestone
- **October 2024** — Server rated in top 2% of Discord communities

> [!quote] Discord Source
> **Author:** oaklandmatt — **Channel:** #general-talk
> We hit 2000 people on the discord! I appreciate all of you so much for helping to create such a beautiful and rich community.

> [!quote] Discord Source

### Community Profiles

austenballard launched the Community Profiles article series, writing in-depth features on server members — starting with Slow Hand and expanding to other contributors.

### Live Streams

oaklandmatt's live streams are the server's anchor event:
- Regular sessions covering mixing, production, and career topics
- Slow Hand provides detailed show notes for every stream
- Streams generate follow-up discussions that fuel the Discord channels

### Culture of Gratitude

The community has an unusually strong culture of public appreciation:
- Holiday messages from oaklandmatt consistently receive 90+ reactions
- Members openly thank each other for advice that improved their work
- The server's "no self-promotion, no politics" rules keep focus on mutual support
